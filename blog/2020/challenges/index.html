Importance of challenging yourself.

Published: 25 Jan 2020

So I think we've all been there, stuck in trying to improve - be it in sports, arts, school, programming or competitively eating jars of mayonnaise.

What do ?

Lets not pretend I am qualified to give anyone any advice on this, I did not go to school for that - hell lets not pretend anyone will ever read this. But hey, I can write down what works for me, to atleast remind myself of it - words have been spoken and this is online afterall.

So what works for me ?

Whenever I feel I am stuck progressing something - and that happens frequently while programming, drawing or playing guitar. I try to form a realistic goal, and this is where all that college money finally pays off - I formulate it SMART, Specific Measurable Acceptable Realistic Timebased.

So try to form a challenge for yourself, but be fair (make sure it is attainable and realistic) and specific, so you have a clear scope of what you will and wont do before you start. If halfway through you get a burst of creativity and want to do extra, sure - aslong as its within the time restraints you set for yourself. That is why most of my study pens could be more 'polished' - I rather learn something new then spend hours upon hours trying to polish off study material.

But I need inspiration first

This is often the problem for a lot of people, without inspiration it is hard to push yourself to start creating something - Hard to create something if you can not fill in that 'something'.

This is where daily/weekly challenges come into play. There are a lot of different communities that setup small 'competitions' for learning, they usually give you a subject or goal.

If you are uncomfortable joining these communities for w.e. reason, you can still use them as a source of inspiration, and just practise them on your own.

Here are some communities that work for me.

Programming

  • /r/dailyprogrammer
  • Code Wars

Pixel Art

  • Pixel Dailies
  • Pixel Joint

Courses and Tutorials

There are hours upon hours of courses you can take online to perfect your craft, udemy is a great resource for courses in my opinion - but so is Youtube. You can find 'anything' you want online, so just checking out some tutorials in something you are interested in might spark that inspiration!

That is it for this one, if you notice any mistakes or just want to leave me a message, leave a comment below.
